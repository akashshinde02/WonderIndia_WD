Top Destinations

1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, built in 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Its white marble beauty symbolizes eternal love and status of the sacrifise.

Explore More Plan Trip

2. Red Fort

The Red Fort, built in 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan, is a stunning red sandstone fortress in Delhi. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it stands as a symbol of India’s independence and Mughal grandeur and one of the great monument.

Explore More Plan Trip

3. Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, built in 1604 by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, is the holiest shrine of Sikhism. Located in Amritsar, it shines with a gold-plated dome and is surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar. Famous for its Langar, it welcomes everyone with warmth and peace.

Explore More Plan Trip

4. Amer Fort

Amer Fort, built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, is a stunning fortress near Jaipur. Known for its blend of Rajput and Mughal styles, the fort features the dazzling Sheesh Mahal, vast courtyards, and artistic carvings. Its red sandstone and marble structure overlooks the serene Maota Lake.

Explore More Plan Trip

5. Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or the Palace of Winds, was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Constructed with red and pink sandstone, it has 953 jharokhas (small windows) designed for royal women to watch daily life without being seen. Its unique crown-like structure makes it a symbol of Jaipur.

Explore More Plan Trip

6. Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, completed in 1912, is the royal residence of the Wadiyars of Mysore. Famous for its Indo-Saracenic architecture, the palace glows with over 100,000 lights during Dasara, creating a stunning spectacle. It remains one of India’s most visited monuments and one of the great place to visit.
